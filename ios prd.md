I am building an iOS app called AI Student Helper using SwiftUI and Swift in Xcode. The app is the iOS version of my existing web app which uses Python Flask, PostgreSQL on Neon Cloud, Google OAuth, and 5 AI models including Groq Llama 3, Gemini, Kimi, Mistral Large, and Gemma 2B. The iOS app must have the exact same pages and data as the web version.
Build all 9 screens using SwiftUI with this design system: dark background color #0a0a0a, card background #111720, teal accent color #0D7377, white text, gray subtext, Inter or SF Pro font. Clean minimal design, no gradients, no animations, centered card layouts.
Here are all 9 screens to build:
Screen 1 — Landing Screen. First screen anyone sees before logging in. Shows the app logo icon, the app name AI Student Helper in large bold white text, a tagline in gray that says Your AI-powered study companion, a Sign in with Google button in teal, and a Create Account button with dark outline below it.
Screen 2 — Sign In Screen. Shows the app name at top, a Continue with Google button, a divider that says or sign in with email, an email text field, a password text field with show and hide toggle, a Sign In button in teal, a Forgot password link, and a link at the bottom that says Don't have an account? Create one for free.
Screen 3 — Sign Up Screen. Shows a Sign Up with Google button, a divider that says or create with email, first name and last name fields side by side, an email field, a password field, a confirm password field, a Create Account button in teal, and a link that says Already have an account? Sign in.
Screen 4 — Welcome Screen. Shows once after first login. Shows a teal checkmark icon, a large welcome message with the student's first name, a short gray line that says You are all set, and a single Go to Dashboard button in teal.
Screen 5 — Setup Screen. Shows once after Google login for first time users. Shows a heading that says Tell us your name, a first name field and last name field, a subject picker with options Math, Science, Computer Science, History, English, Other, and a Get Started button in teal.
Screen 6 — Dashboard Screen. Main chat interface. Top navigation bar shows the app logo on left and profile avatar on right. Below nav bar are two dropdown selectors — subject selector and AI model selector showing Groq Llama 3, Gemini, Kimi, Mistral Large, Gemma 2B. Main area shows chat bubbles — student messages on right in teal rounded bubble, AI answers on left in dark gray rounded bubble with subject tag and model name shown above each answer. At bottom is a large text input field with a send button as a round teal arrow button. Top right has Clear Chat and New Chat buttons.
Screen 7 — History Screen. Shows Chat History title at top with a search bar below it. Subject filter pills — All, Math, Science, Computer Science, History, English, Other. Below that a scrollable list of conversation cards. Each card shows the subject tag in teal, the question in white bold, the AI answer preview in gray, the model name, and the date on the right.
Screen 8 — Profile Screen. Shows a circular avatar with the user's initials at top, full name in large white text, Google email in gray, and a Signed in with Google badge. Below that a learning statistics section showing Total Chats, Questions Asked, Subjects Explored, and Day Streak in a 2x2 grid with teal numbers. Below that account details section with name and email fields and an Edit button. A Sign Out button in red at the top right corner.
Screen 9 — Settings Screen. Three sections. First section called Learning Preferences has a default subject dropdown and preferred AI model selector with all 5 models listed as cards showing model name and description. Second section called Appearance has a dark mode toggle, compact messages toggle, and font size selector. Third section called Data and Privacy has a Clear History button and a Delete Account button in red. A Save Changes button in teal at the top right.
Screen 10 — About Screen. Shows the app icon at top, the app name in large bold white, a Version 1.0.0 stable badge. A paragraph about what the app does. A View on GitHub button and a Contact Us button side by side. Built by Mohammad Hussainkhail at the bottom with github.com/modevs-cloud.
Make sure all screens use the same navigation structure with a bottom tab bar for logged in screens containing Chat, History, Profile, Settings, and About tabs. Use NavigationView and proper SwiftUI navigation between screens. All screens must be responsive and work on iPhone 15 Pro screen size.
